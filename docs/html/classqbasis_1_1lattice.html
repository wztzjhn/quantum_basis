Detailed Description

In general, the position of any site can be represented as:

\[ \vec{r} = \sum_{i=1}^{D} m_i^0 \vec{a}_i + \sum_{i=1}^D M_i \vec{A}_i + \vec{d}_s = \sum_{i=1}^{D} m_i \vec{a}_i + \vec{d}_s, \]

where \( m_i^0 \), \( m_i \), and \( M_i \) are integers, and \( \vec{d}_s \) are the position shift of sublattices. Note: \( m_i^0 \) is located in the 1st supercell defined by

\[ \sum_{i=1}^D m_i^0 \vec{a}_i = \sum_{i=1}^D x_i \vec{A}_i, \]

where \( 0 \le x_i < 1 \). In other words, any site has two coordinate labels, one within the supercell, another as the supercell coordinate. Most of time, the supercell coordinate is set at origin; but they can be effective in some other cases.
